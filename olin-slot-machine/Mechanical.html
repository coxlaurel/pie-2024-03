Mechanical Design

The coin slot machine is a four-foot-tall box with a front face angled for user convenience. The machine features three spinning wheels, each displaying five distinct images: four PIE professors and a picture of pie. The mechanical design employs a combination of custom fabrication, precision components, and user-triggered mechanisms to ensure smooth operation.

Box Construction

  • The box is constructed from ½-inch plywood sheets, originally 4 ft x 8 ft, which were cut into 4 ft x 4 ft pieces to create the four main sections of the machine.
  • The front face is angled for ergonomic interaction, while the back is equipped with a lock and hinge for maintenance access. This lock allows the coin slot and internal components to be serviced easily from the back.

Front view of the slot machine's face

Wheel and Gear System

  • Three wheels are mounted on a horizontal dowel that spans the entire machine, acting as the central axle.
  • Ball bearings are used to support each wheel on the dowel, ensuring smooth and frictionless rotation.
  • Bevel gears are attached to the sides of the wheels to enable rotation. These are coupled with bevel gears mounted on vertical shafts of individual motors.

  • The middle motor is mounted on laser-cut supports between the wheels.
  • The left and right motors are secured to the inside walls of the machine using CAD-designed, 3D-printed motor mounts.

Activation and Stopping Mechanism

Activation

The system is activated when a coin is inserted into a coin slot located on the front face of the machine. An infrared (IR) sensor detects the coin’s passage and signals the motors to start spinning the wheels.

Stopping

A lever on the side of the machine is used to stop the wheels. Pulling the lever moves a wooden arm inside the machine. The exposed end of a wire on this arm makes contact with another wire, completing a circuit and signaling the motors to stop spinning.

Fabrication and Custom Components

  • The wheels and supporting structures were laser-cut to achieve precise dimensions and proper alignment.
  • Bevel gears and motor mounts were CAD-designed and 3D-printed for a precise fit and optimal performance.
  • The box’s robust plywood construction provides structural stability, while the lock on the back panel ensures secure and easy access for internal maintenance.
