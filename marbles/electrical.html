Electrical System

The electrical system provides the foundation for the operation of the entire project, integrating power distribution, signal processing, and hardware interfacing. Designed in KiCad, the system is built with robust connections, voltage regulation, and control pathways.

Power Distribution

The power distribution system ensures consistent voltage and current delivery to all components. A 16V power source is used, regulated down to 5V and 3.3V where necessary. Key components include:

      A 16V power source.
        A DC-DC step-down converter for efficient conversion to 5V and 3.3V for low-power peripherals.

Microcontroller and Peripheral Connections

The Arduino Uno is the core of the electrical system, interfacing with multiple peripherals:

      The TCS34725 color sensor communicates using I2C for marble color detection.
        Servo motors are controlled via PWM signals, enabling precise marble sorting.
          A relay module manages high-power components such as the DC motor for the lift system and the solenoid.

Wiring and Layout

The wiring layout emphasizes organization and reliability. Wires are grouped and routed to minimize interference. The KiCad schematic was translated into a physical layout with:

      Color-coded wires for easy identification of power, ground, and signal lines.
        Custom PCB for compact integration of voltage regulation and relay control.
          Screw terminals to secure connections for high-current components.
